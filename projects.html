01
Enterprise App Modernization Omnissa · 2025

Horizon Client

Omnissa (formerly VMware)

A trusted legacy solution in the Omnissa product lineup — redesigned from the ground up. Horizon Client enables end users to connect to virtual desktops from any device, anywhere, across Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, Linux, and Chrome OS.

The challenge

The core problem was the absence of a unified design system. This created inconsistent experiences across platforms and engineering inefficiencies due to the lack of a single source of truth. We needed to understand how these problems affected users before proposing any solution.

Our approach

We began with a comprehensive design audit — reviewing navigation patterns, action consistency, system feedback, and accessibility — using data to establish a clear baseline. We partnered with the Research team to align stakeholders on a research plan focused on real user pain points. In parallel, we proposed a phased design system approach that balanced quick wins with long-term investment, driving immediate improvements while building a scalable, consistent, and accessible experience across all platforms.

Impact

30–40% Reduction in task completion time for IT admin workflows
+15% Usability score improvement from user interviews
~30% Reduction in onboarding and training time
WCAG AA Full compliance achieved, mitigating legal and reputational risks

  • Lead Product Designer
  • Design System Architecture
  • Accessibility Auditing
  • Cross-platform UX
  • Stakeholder Alignment

02
Civic Tech Government Services Deloitte · 2022

Colorado Peak

Deloitte Consulting — Internal Pitch

Reimagining how citizens of Colorado access critical state services — benefits, healthcare, employment — through a more human-centered, connected system built on accessibility, plain language, and trust.

The challenge

Colorado's Peak platform served millions of citizens navigating complex benefit systems, but the legacy interface created unnecessary friction and confusion. Case workers spent significant time helping users with basic navigation rather than providing substantive support.

Our approach

We conducted extensive field research with case workers and citizens — identifying real pain points, language barriers, and accessibility gaps in the existing system. From this foundation, we proposed a unified service portal that reduced cognitive load, used plain language throughout, and met WCAG AA standards. The design centered equity: ensuring that citizens most in need could access services without friction or fear.

Methods

03
Healthcare AI · Wellness Personal · 2025

Bewell

Personal — Concept Application

An AI-powered wellness hub that unifies fragmented health data — wearables, lab results, medications, and mental health check-ins — into one clear, actionable dashboard. Designed for progressive disclosure, privacy, and daily habit formation.

Design highlights

  • Personalised health score dashboard with contextual interpretation
  • AI-generated insight summaries in plain language, not medical jargon
  • Habit tracker with contextual reminders based on patterns, not schedules
  • Progressive disclosure — complexity available when needed, hidden when not
  • Full WCAG AA compliance throughout

Key decisions

  • Gentle nudges over overwhelming metrics
  • Privacy-first data model
  • Accessible colour system for health status
  • Single daily summary, not a stream of notifications
04
Healthcare Emergency Triage Deloitte · 2017

Bloom

Deloitte — Internal Pitch

A concept application that simplifies emergency triage for clinicians while keeping patients and their families informed in real time. Designed for high-stress, high-speed environments without sacrificing clinical accuracy.

Design highlights

  • Real-time patient status board — at-a-glance triage priority
  • Smart triage intake flow with clinical decision support
  • Family-facing status updates that reduce nurse interruptions
  • Real-time handoff summaries to reduce error in shift transitions
  • Designed for glanceability under pressure

Design principles

  • Glanceability over completeness
  • One primary action per screen
  • High-contrast for clinical environments
  • Reduce cognitive load under stress
